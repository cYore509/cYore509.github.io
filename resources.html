Resources

Find helpful tools, guides, and programs to support your journey.

This page contains useful resources for survivors meant to help for all stages of the journey

Treatment

  • ACS ACTS is a program which empowers survivors to find the best treatment available for them. This is done through a personalized clinical trial matching program where survivors are informed on available trials and connected to specialists to find one which works with each survivor.
  • Hope Lodge is available to survivors undergoing treatments which are away from their homes. Allowing survivors to have a place to stay gives them the opportunity to have access to better treatment while not bearing the financial pressure of being away from home paying for expenses. Hope Lodges provide a free place to stay during treatment so people with cancer can better focus on getting better.
  • Road to Recovery was a favorite program of Liza's. It offers a minimal though significant ease of stress for patients treatment days. Making sure any patient has the ability to get to necessary treatment at no cost to the patient leaves a strong impact on those tough days.

Survivors

  • The Cancer Survivors Networkis a peer support community for cancer patients, survivors, caregivers, families, and friends! CSN is a safe place to connect with others who share your interests and experiences. Here for all to make sure you never feel alone or without a friend.
  • Specific to those fighting breast cancer, the American Cancer Society Reach To Recovery program connects people facing breast cancer - from diagnosis through survivorship with one on one support with survivors to help those facing the journey cope with the disease.

Cancer Helpline

  • ACS's 24/7 cancer helpline provides support for people dealing with cancer. It connects you with trained cancer information specialists who will answer questions and provide guidance as well as a compassionate ear for callers.
  • Call them at 1-800-227-2345
